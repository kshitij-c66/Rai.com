राय · RAI: New Governing Movement

हिन्द की जीत, हिन्द की रीत
(Hind ki Jeet, Hind ki Reet)

On the historic date of 26 January 2026, the RAI movement was formally established in a quiet assembly by the banks of the Ganga. Not as a political party, not as a militia — but as a renaissance of India's eternal governing principles. RAI draws belief from the courage of the Indian National Army, yet it stands as an independent sovereign current: a body of citizens, intellectuals, and custodians of dharma who envision a new order rooted in swaraj and civilizational ethos. Its absolute motto, "Hind ki Jeet, Hind ki Reet", proclaims that victory for India shall be achieved through India's own traditions, customs, and ethical governance.

RAI assemblence in session, 2026 (conceptual illustration).
Not a unit — but a markable force for governance

RAI does not seek to be a regiment; it seeks to imprint a governing style rooted in consultation (raiyat) and justice for those who have no voice. It fights for the voiceless—the peasant, the artisan, the displaced—and strives to replace the current structure with a system that reflects India's soul. Its members are volunteers of the pen, the plow, and the podium. RAI was established in 2026 precisely to address the growing disconnect between the rulers and the ruled. Its founders, coming from diverse backgrounds, signed the "Ganga Compact" — a pledge to rebuild governance from the village up.

Mission: Transform governance, restore reet

RAI's mission is to establish a government that answers to the people — not through borrowed western templates, but through the reet (custom) of India: panchayats, consensus, and accountability. We believe the current government, despite its claims, perpetuates colonial structures. RAI therefore works tirelessly to pass a new governing charter, one that ensures power flows from village assemblies to the centre. We fight for those who cannot fight: the landless, the marginalised, the silent millions whose toil builds the nation.

Our pledge: To replace the present system with a governance that is truly indigenous, transparent, and just. RAI is not a party; it is a movement of conscience. Every individual who joins takes the oath: "I shall work for Hind ki Jeet through Hind ki Reet."
